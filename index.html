Okayama Korakuen Garden

This traditional Japanese garden was created nearly 300 years ago by the daimyo (feudal lord of the domain) of the region. Symbolizing the power of the samurai, Korakuen Garden in Okayama is considered one of the three most beautiful gardens in Japan along with Kenroku-en Garden in Kanazawa and Kairakuen Garden in Mito. Located around the Eny√¥-tei house, which was built as the main residential building, the garden covers an area of 13 hectares and includes a Noh theater as well as ponds, a hill, plum groves and a tea plantation. The garden is laid out with large expanses of grass showing an openness not often seen in Japanese gardens. It is a kaiyu (walking garden) style garden that allows visitors to enjoy the varied landscape as they stroll through the garden, especially at the top of Yuishinzan Hill and along the nearby streams.

One of the three best-known gardens in Japan, having been awarded 3 stars in the Michelin Green Guide to Japan
